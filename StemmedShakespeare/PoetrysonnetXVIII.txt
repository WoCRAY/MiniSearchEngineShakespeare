sonnet xviii sonnet xviii shall i compar thee to a summer day thou art more love and more temper rough wind do shake the darl bud of may and summer leas hath all too short a date sometim too hot the eye of heaven shine and often is his gold complexion dimm'd and everi fair from fair sometim declin by chanc or natur chang cours untrimm'd but thi etern summer shall not fade nor lose possess of that fair thou owest nor shall death brag thou wander'st in his shade when in etern line to time thou growest so long as men can breath or eye can see so long live this and this give life to thee
